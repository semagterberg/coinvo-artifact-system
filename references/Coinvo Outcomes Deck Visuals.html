A side-by-side visualization comparing focused versus scattered interest clusters. On the left, labeled FOCUSED, a dense ring of cyan dots representing accounts forms a tight cluster around the words INTEREST CLUSTER in the center. Connecting lines weave through the cluster creating a strong network. From the bottom of the cluster, many converging lines stream into a content funnel below. A green glow under the funnel indicates STRONG DISTRIBUTION SIGNAL. On the right, labeled SCATTERED, eight small disconnected pods of cyan dots are spread across the area around the same INTEREST CLUSTER label. Thin weak dashed lines connect each pod to the content funnel. No glow under the funnel. The result is labeled WEAK DISTRIBUTION SIGNAL in red. The point: same content, different audience alignment, dramatically different distribution.

Focused vs scattered
The algorithm reads your audience as a cluster signal. One tight interest cluster funnels content cleanly. A scattered audience splits the signal so thin nothing breaks through.
