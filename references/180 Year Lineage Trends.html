A vertical timeline of 7 distribution empires founded between 1846 and 2026: AP, Reuters, BBC, Bloomberg, Politico, Axios, and Coinvo. Each empire is one inheritor in a 180-year lineage of catching existing demand at scale. Coinvo is the next entry in the line, not the experiment.

The 180-year lineage
Six distribution empires across 180 years. Coinvo is the next entry, not the experiment.
1846
Associated Press
Speed wire. The founding act of distribution as an industry, catching news demand at telegraph speed.
Still operating · 180 years
1851
Reuters
First global news network. Paul Julius Reuter built the wire across the Channel and never stopped scaling.
Thomson Reuters · 77B+ market cap
1922
BBC
Broadcast model. The first distribution platform with a public charter, the model copied by every national broadcaster.
Still operating · 104 years
1981
Bloomberg
Terminal aggregation. Built distribution into a closed network of paying professionals, the entire model still runs on it.
40B+ private valuation
2007
Politico
Niche dominance. First digital-native distribution brand to win a vertical so completely the price of entry became the brand name itself.
~1B exit · Axel Springer 2021
2017
Axios
Format velocity. Bullet-point distribution at email speed, the first newsroom to engineer for the attention compression of the 2010s.
525M exit · Cox 2022
2026
Coinvo
AI-speed crypto distribution. Catches recurring demand around BTC, ETH, ETF flows, regulatory moves, faster than any single creator can react.
